# **North American Mobile Monetization Benchmarks: A Comparative Analysis of Mid-Core Gaming and Fitness Subscriptions for 2024-2025**

## **Section 1: Executive Summary**

The North American mobile application market demonstrated a significant return to growth and strategic maturity in 2024, a trend projected to continue through 2025\. After a period of post-pandemic normalization, consumer spending has rebounded, particularly in mature Western markets. This report provides a granular analysis of monetization benchmarks for two distinct, high-value categories within the North American market: Mid-core Mobile Gaming and subscription-based Mobile Fitness applications. The findings are based on the latest available data from 2024 and forecasts for 2025, synthesized from leading industry sources including AppsFlyer, RevenueCat, and Unity.

Key findings indicate that while both sectors are thriving, they operate under fundamentally different economic models. For Mid-core gaming, success is dictated by a "whale-driven" economy. The Day 90 Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) for top-performing iOS titles can reach as high as $9.69 with a hybrid monetization model, yet the underlying conversion rate to a first-time in-app purchase (IAP) remains exceptionally low at just 1.83%. This model's viability hinges on identifying and extracting maximum value from a very small cohort of high-spending players.

Conversely, the Mobile Fitness category exemplifies a "high-intent conversion" model. In North America, this sector demonstrates a best-in-class subscription funnel, converting an impressive 9.4% of all downloads into paying subscribers within 30 days.1 This high conversion rate, coupled with a preference for annual plans, results in the highest median 12-month realized payer Lifetime Value (LTV) of any app category, at $57.25.

Core strategic insights reveal a market-wide pivot from user acquisition volume to user value optimization. The rising cost of acquiring users has rendered pure install-based campaigns inefficient, forcing a strategic shift toward maximizing Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) and LTV. Hybrid monetization—the blending of IAP, in-app advertising (IAA), and subscriptions—has become a foundational strategy, though its application is highly platform-dependent. Analysis shows that hybrid models deliver superior ROAS for Mid-core games on Android, while IAP-only strategies remain dominant on iOS, highlighting two distinct user economies.

Looking ahead to 2025, the market will see a deepening focus on payer retention and value maximization. Key trends will include the expanded use of AI for personalized monetization, the strategic integration of rewarded ad formats to complement primary revenue streams, and a greater emphasis on live-ops and engagement mechanics to combat churn and subscription fatigue. This report provides the definitive benchmarks and strategic analysis required for developers and publishers to navigate this competitive and lucrative landscape.

## **Section 2: The 2024-2025 North American Mobile App Landscape: A Macro View**

### **2.1 Market Stabilization and a Return to Growth**

The period of 2024-2025 marks a crucial phase of stabilization and renewed growth for the global mobile app market, with North America standing out as a primary engine of this recovery. Following a market correction in 2022 and 2023 that saw a cooling of the explosive growth experienced during the pandemic, 2024 demonstrated a return to a positive trajectory. Global gaming revenue reached approximately $187.7 billion in 2024, a year-over-year (YoY) increase of about 2%.

This growth is not evenly distributed; it is highly concentrated in mature Western markets. The North American gaming market, specifically, expanded to $50.6 billion in 2024, representing a 1.7% YoY increase. More pointedly, data from Sensor Tower reveals that mobile game in-app purchase (IAP) revenue in North America surged by 9% in 2024, a significant rebound that underscores the region's robust consumer spending power. In total, IAP revenue across all app categories in the United States alone reached a remarkable $52 billion in 2024, cementing its position as the world's leading market for mobile monetization. This concentration of high-value consumers makes the North American market a critical focus for any monetization strategy. However, this revenue growth occurs within a complex landscape where user acquisition trends are diverging. While spending is up, global mobile game downloads declined by 6% YoY in 2024, indicating that the market's expansion is not being driven by a growing user base but by deeper monetization of existing and newly acquired, high-value players.

### **2.2 The Strategic Pivot from CPI to LTV/ROAS**

The current market dynamics have catalyzed a fundamental shift in user acquisition (UA) strategy. The era of prioritizing cheap installs at scale has given way to a more sophisticated, value-centric approach. The market's maturity, combined with high competition, has driven up acquisition costs, making a focus on Lifetime Value (LTV) and Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) a financial necessity. The average Cost Per Install (CPI) for a mobile game in North America stands at a considerable $3.59, compelling publishers to ensure that each acquired user generates sufficient revenue to justify this initial investment.

In response, leading advertisers are actively diversifying their UA strategies. Data from Unity's 2024 "Mobile Growth and Monetization Report" indicates a clear trend of advertisers moving away from pure CPI-based campaigns. Instead, budgets are being allocated toward ROAS and event-based optimization campaigns, which are designed to target and acquire users who are more likely to perform high-value in-app actions, such as completing a tutorial, reaching a certain level, or making an IAP. This strategic pivot is further evidenced by the disproportionate growth in remarketing efforts. Paid remarketing conversions grew by an impressive 22% YoY in 2024, a rate ten times faster than the 2% growth seen in new paid installs. This highlights a clear industry-wide focus on maximizing the value of the existing user base and re-engaging lapsed users, rather than solely relying on a constant influx of new installs. This disciplined, data-driven approach to UA is the defining characteristic of successful operators in the current landscape.

### **2.3 The Hybridization Imperative**

The evolution of monetization strategy has moved in lockstep with the changes in user acquisition. Hybrid monetization—the practice of combining multiple revenue streams such as IAP, IAA, subscriptions, and one-time purchases—is no longer an emerging trend but a core, foundational strategy for a growing number of applications. This approach is a direct response to the economic reality of the freemium model: an extremely small fraction of users will ever make a direct purchase. Data from Unity indicates that only 1.83% of mobile gamers make an IAP, leaving over 98% of the user base unmonetized through direct spending.

Hybrid models are designed to capture value from this non-paying majority. In-app advertising revenue in gaming grew by 7% YoY in 2024, a surge largely attributed to the adoption of hybrid strategies that integrate rewarded video and other ad formats alongside IAP economies. This trend extends well beyond gaming. RevenueCat's 2025 "State of Subscription Apps" report reveals that 35% of all subscription-based apps now incorporate other monetization methods, such as one-time purchases for consumables or feature unlocks. Gaming apps are at the forefront of this movement, with 61.7% of them employing a mixed-monetization approach. This blurring of traditional monetization lines allows publishers to build more resilient and profitable economic models that cater to a wider spectrum of user behaviors and spending propensities, maximizing the total revenue potential of their entire user base.

### **2.4 The Great Divide: iOS vs. Android Monetization**

Despite the global reach of both major mobile platforms, a persistent and significant performance gap in monetization exists between iOS and Android users, particularly in high-income markets like North America. This economic disparity is a critical factor that must inform every aspect of a developer's strategy, from initial development choices to UA budget allocation and revenue forecasting.

The difference is most stark in the subscription economy. An analysis by AppsFlyer found that the Day 90 ARPU for non-gaming subscription apps on iOS was $8.39, more than five times greater than the $1.54 generated on Android. This trend holds for early revenue generation as well. Data from RevenueCat shows that the median 60-day Revenue Per Install (a metric similar to ARPU) for all apps in North America is $0.66 on the App Store, nearly double the $0.35 seen on Google Play.2 This fundamental difference in user spending behavior means that a "one-size-fits-all" approach to monetization is destined for suboptimal results. As will be explored in subsequent sections, this platform divide dictates not only different revenue expectations but entirely different strategic playbooks, particularly for genres like Mid-core gaming where monetization models can be tailored with high precision.

## **Section 3: Deep Dive: Monetization Benchmarks for Mid-Core Mobile Gaming**

The Mid-core gaming category, which includes genres like Fighting, Strategy, and RPGs, operates under a unique and challenging economic model. Success in this segment is predicated on attracting and retaining a highly engaged user base willing to invest significant time and money into a deep, complex game experience. The following benchmarks for the North American market in 2024-2025 reveal an economy driven by a small but extremely valuable cohort of paying players.

### **3.1 IAP Revenue and Conversion Dynamics**

While the broader mobile gaming market saw a healthy 4% YoY growth in IAP revenue in 2024, the Mid-core genre experienced a slight contraction, with IAP revenue declining by 2%.3 This suggests that Mid-core titles are facing increased competition for consumer spending, possibly from breakout hits in the rapidly growing hybrid-casual space. In this competitive environment, understanding the conversion funnel is paramount.

The single most critical benchmark for any freemium Mid-core title is the conversion rate to a first-time purchase. According to a September 2024 report from Unity, on average, **only 1.83% of all mobile game players ever make an in-app purchase**. Other analyses place this figure in the range of 1-3% of *daily active users*. This extremely low conversion rate establishes the fundamental challenge of the model: the vast majority of players will never directly contribute to IAP revenue.

The challenge extends beyond securing the first purchase. Of the small minority of players who do convert, data shows that only **28.81% are likely to make a subsequent purchase**. The timing of this second purchase is critical. The same Unity report reveals that a staggering **92% of all repeat purchases occur within the first 30 days** of the player's initial transaction. This creates a narrow but vital window for developers. The period immediately following a user's first payment (often corresponding to the "mid-game" phase, from Day 8 to Day 15\) is the most crucial time to nurture that player with targeted offers, valuable content, and compelling reasons to invest further, thereby converting them from a one-time buyer into a high-value, repeat spender.

### **3.2 User Value Benchmarks: ARPU and ARPPU**

Given the low conversion rates, measuring the average revenue generated per user (ARPU) and per paying user (ARPPU) is essential for evaluating monetization efficiency and guiding UA strategy.

Day 90 ARPU serves as a key performance indicator for the medium-term value of an acquired user. Data from AppsFlyer's Q3 2024 analysis of high-income markets, including North America, provides a detailed breakdown for Mid-core games, revealing significant differences based on both platform and monetization model:

* **iOS (IAP-only):** $7.31  
* **iOS (Hybrid Monetization):** $9.69  
* **Android (IAP-only):** $3.11  
* **Android (Hybrid Monetization):** $1.54

These figures highlight the superior monetization potential of the iOS user base for Mid-core titles. While specific 2024 ARPPU figures for the genre are not widely published, qualitative data provides a clear directional understanding. AppsFlyer notes that iOS captures approximately 60% of all IAP revenue in Mid-core games, indicating that the value of a paying user—the "whale"—is substantially higher on iOS than on Android.

A powerful top-down benchmark from a collaborative report by Newzoo and Tebex (published August 2025, reflecting 2024-2025 data) offers a proxy for annual ARPPU. The report finds that **the average North American paying gamer spends $324.90 annually** across all gaming platforms. Given that mobile gaming revenue is generated almost entirely from in-game spending, this figure provides an excellent benchmark for the annual revenue potential of a committed, paying player in a Mid-core game. This juxtaposition of a low overall conversion rate with a high annual spend per payer confirms that the Mid-core business model is not about monetizing the masses, but about maximizing the value of a select few.

### **3.3 The Strategic Impact of Hybrid Monetization on ROAS**

The most telling data for Mid-core strategy comes from analyzing the Day 90 ROAS, which measures the revenue generated from a user cohort against the cost of acquiring them. Here, the data reveals two completely different strategic imperatives for iOS and Android. The following benchmarks are from AppsFlyer's Q3 2024 analysis of high-income markets.

For **Android**, a hybrid monetization strategy is definitively superior.

* **Hybrid ROAS (Day 90):** 146%  
* **IAP-only ROAS (Day 90):** 93%  
* **IAA-only ROAS (Day 90):** 58%

The data provides a clear directive: on Android, integrating IAA alongside IAP generates a 57% higher return on ad spend compared to an IAP-only approach. This suggests that for the broader, more price-sensitive Android audience, monetizing non-payers through ads is crucial to achieving a profitable blended ROAS.

For **iOS**, the dynamic is completely inverted, favoring a pure IAP strategy.

* **IAP-only ROAS (Day 90):** 215%  
* **Hybrid ROAS (Day 90):** 73%

This stark contrast indicates that for the high-value iOS user base, an IAP-only model is vastly more profitable. The introduction of in-app advertising appears to cannibalize IAP revenue or otherwise disrupt the experience for high-spending players, leading to a significantly lower overall return. This platform-specific divergence is arguably the single most important strategic consideration for any Mid-core developer operating in the North American market. The optimal monetization strategy is not determined by the game's genre, but by the platform on which it is deployed.

## **Section 4: Deep Dive: Monetization Benchmarks for Subscription-Based Fitness Apps**

The Health & Fitness app category represents a best-in-class example of a successful subscription business model in the mobile ecosystem. Unlike the low-conversion, high-spend dynamics of Mid-core gaming, fitness apps thrive by converting a significant percentage of users with high intrinsic motivation into predictable, recurring revenue streams. The benchmarks for North America, drawn primarily from RevenueCat's authoritative "State of Subscription Apps" reports for 2024 and 2025, illustrate a powerful and efficient monetization funnel.

### **4.1 The Subscription Conversion Funnel: A North American Powerhouse**

The success of fitness apps begins at the top of the funnel, where they effectively convert user intent into engagement and, ultimately, payment. The user journey is marked by strong performance at each stage.

* **Install-to-Trial Start Rate:** The first critical conversion point is convincing a new user to begin a free trial. For the Health & Fitness category, the median trial start rate is a strong **6.7%**. This indicates that users who download these apps are not merely browsing; they arrive with a pre-existing motivation for self-improvement and are actively seeking a solution. Top-quartile applications in this category achieve an even more impressive trial start rate of **13.5%**, demonstrating the power of a compelling onboarding experience and clear value proposition.  
* **Trial-to-Paid Conversion Rate:** The second, and most crucial, conversion is from a trial user to a paying subscriber. Here, Health & Fitness apps excel, with a median conversion rate of **44.5%**. This high rate suggests that the trial periods are effective at demonstrating the app's core value and convincing users of its long-term benefits. The best-performing apps in the upper quartile convert at an exceptional rate of **58.8%**, setting a high bar for the industry.  
* **Download-to-Paid Conversion Rate (30-Day):** The blended result of these two stages is the overall download-to-paid conversion rate, a key metric for forecasting and UA budgeting. In the uniquely lucrative North American market, Health & Fitness apps convert a median of **9.4% of all downloads into paying subscribers within their first 30 days**.1 This figure is exceptionally high, starkly contrasting with the global all-category average of just 1.7%, and it underscores the powerful product-market fit that fitness apps have achieved in this region.1

### **4.2 User Value Benchmarks: ARPU and Payer LTV**

High conversion rates are matched by strong revenue generation, both in the short term and over the long-term lifetime of a subscriber.

* **Average Revenue Per Install (ARPI):** This metric, also known as ARPU, measures the average revenue generated from every user who installs the app, providing a blended view of payers and non-payers. It is a critical metric for optimizing UA spend. According to RevenueCat's 2025 report, Health & Fitness leads all non-gaming categories in early monetization.2  
  * **14-Day ARPI (Median): $0.44**. The upper quartile reaches $1.31, indicating that top apps monetize extremely quickly and efficiently.  
  * **60-Day ARPI (Median): $0.63**. This figure matches AI apps for the highest among all categories and sits above the overall North American median of $0.60, confirming the category's top-tier performance.2  
* **Realized Payer LTV:** This is the ultimate measure of monetization success, tracking the total revenue generated from a paying subscriber over their lifetime with the app. Data from RevenueCat's 2025 report shows that Health & Fitness is the undisputed leader in long-term subscriber value.2  
  * **1-Month Realized Payer LTV (Median): $13.13**  
  * **12-Month Realized Payer LTV (Median): $57.25**  
  * **12-Month Realized Payer LTV (90th Percentile): $404.75**

The gap between the median and the top 10% (P90) is immense, with top-performing apps generating seven times more revenue per payer over a year than the average app. This power-law distribution indicates that while the category average is strong, exceptional success is driven by superior retention, upselling, and long-term engagement strategies that create highly valuable, multi-year subscribers.

### **4.3 Subscription Strategy: Pricing and Retention**

The high LTV of fitness apps is structurally supported by a strategic focus on long-term commitments and user retention.

* **Plan Duration and Pricing:** The dominant subscription model in Health & Fitness is the annual plan, which accounts for **67% of all subscriptions** in the category. This aligns with the nature of fitness goals, which require long-term commitment, and allows publishers to secure a full year of revenue upfront. While pricing varies, the most common price points across the broader subscription market have standardized around $9.99 per month and $29.99 per year. However, the average price for a monthly subscription has increased by 14% YoY to $8.01, indicating a general upward trend in pricing. Fitness apps often command premium pricing above these common points due to their high perceived value.  
* **Retention Benchmarks:** Subscriber retention is the cornerstone of a profitable subscription business. While annual plans offer superior retention, the market is showing signs of increased churn, often referred to as "subscription fatigue."  
  * The **1-year retention rate for annual plans** across all categories is **44.1%**, a slight decrease from 47.1% in the previous period.  
  * The **1-year retention rate for monthly plans** is significantly lower at **17.0%**, down from 18.8%.

This modest but notable decline in retention across all plan types highlights a growing challenge for publishers. It is no longer enough to simply acquire a subscriber; maintaining their engagement and continuously demonstrating value throughout the subscription term is now more critical than ever to secure the renewal and maximize LTV.

## **Section 5: Comparative Analysis and Strategic Implications**

A direct comparison of the monetization models for Mid-core Gaming and subscription-based Fitness apps reveals two distinct paths to profitability in the North American mobile market. While both are highly successful, their underlying economics, user behaviors, and strategic imperatives are fundamentally different. Understanding these differences allows for a more nuanced approach to strategy and highlights opportunities for cross-pollination of tactics.

### **5.1 Juxtaposing Monetization Models**

The Mid-core Gaming model can be characterized as **"whale hunting."** It is an economic system built on a massive base of non-paying users, from which it must identify and convert an extremely small minority (1.83%) into payers. The business is not sustained by the average user, but by the disproportionately high spending of this tiny cohort, with top players in North America spending over $300 annually. This creates a high-risk, high-reward environment where the primary strategic challenge is the efficient identification and long-term retention of these "whales." Success is measured by metrics like ARPPU and ROAS, and UA strategies are finely tuned to find users who exhibit behaviors correlated with high spending potential.

In contrast, the Fitness Subscription model is one of **"high-intent conversion."** It targets users who already possess a strong, intrinsic motivation for self-improvement. This high intent translates directly into a much higher conversion rate through the funnel, with 9.4% of North American downloads becoming paying subscribers.1 The revenue stream is not driven by impulse buys but by a deliberate commitment to a recurring, predictable payment, typically on an annual basis. The strategic challenge here is not finding the rare spender in a sea of free users, but rather effectively demonstrating the app's value during a trial period to convert a motivated user into a long-term, loyal subscriber. Success is measured by metrics like trial-to-paid conversion rate and realized payer LTV.

### **5.2 Cross-Pollination of Strategies**

Despite their differences, each model contains successful tactics that can be adapted by the other to address inherent weaknesses.

* **What Gaming Can Learn from Fitness:** The primary lesson is the power of a structured, value-driven trial experience. The fitness app funnel excels at converting users by providing a tangible taste of the premium experience, leading to a 44.5% trial-to-paid conversion rate. While many Mid-core games have gated freemium models, they could benefit from experimenting with time-limited trials that grant full access to premium features. This approach could more effectively demonstrate the value of IAPs and potentially increase the initial 1.83% conversion rate by creating a clearer path to understanding the benefits of spending.  
* **What Fitness Can Learn from Gaming:** The key takeaway is the mastery of long-term engagement through live operations ("live-ops"). Mid-core games are experts at retaining users with daily rewards, login bonuses, special events, competitive leaderboards, and social mechanics. As fitness apps face the challenge of declining retention rates and subscription fatigue, adopting these gaming mechanics can be a powerful tool. Implementing daily check-ins, weekly workout challenges, or community-based goals can build habits, increase session frequency, and provide continuous value that justifies an annual subscription renewal. Furthermore, the trend toward hybrid monetization in gaming suggests an opportunity for fitness apps to supplement their core subscription with one-time purchases, such as specialized training programs or premium content bundles, thereby increasing total LTV.

### **5.3 The Definitive Platform Playbook**

The most critical strategic implication derived from the data is that monetization strategy must be tailored to the specific economic environment of each platform. The performance differences between iOS and Android are not minor variations but represent two distinct markets that demand different approaches.

For **Mid-core Gaming**, the playbook is starkly bifurcated. On iOS, the data overwhelmingly supports an **IAP-only strategy**. The 215% D90 ROAS for IAP-only models is triple that of hybrid models, indicating that the high-value iOS player base responds best to a pure, uninterrupted premium experience. On **Android**, the opposite is true. A **hybrid monetization model is essential**, as it delivers a 146% D90 ROAS compared to just 93% for IAP-only. The strategy must be to monetize the large non-paying audience with ads to achieve profitability.

For **Fitness Apps**, the subscription model is viable on both platforms. However, the significantly higher ARPI on iOS ($0.66 60-day median vs. $0.35 on Android) means that LTV targets and allowable Customer Acquisition Costs (CAC) must be adjusted accordingly.2 The strategy is less about a different monetization model and more about platform-specific forecasting and UA budget allocation.

## **Section 6: Future Outlook and Strategic Recommendations for 2025 and Beyond**

### **6.1 Projected Market Trajectory**

The outlook for both the mobile gaming and fitness app markets remains positive, with continued growth projected for 2025 and beyond. The global mobile gaming market is forecast to approach the $200 billion revenue mark in 2025, continuing its modest but steady expansion. The mobile fitness app market is on an even more aggressive growth path, with a projected Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 13.59% through 2033, driven by increasing health consciousness and technological integration. Within this global context, North America is expected to maintain its position as the most valuable and lucrative region for in-app purchases and subscription revenue, making it a continued priority for top-tier publishers.

### **6.2 Emerging Monetization Trends**

Several key trends are poised to shape monetization strategies in the coming years:

* **AI-Driven Personalization:** The integration of Artificial Intelligence will move beyond workflow optimization and become a central pillar of monetization. AI will enable hyper-personalization at scale, allowing apps to dynamically price IAPs, generate personalized offers for users at risk of churning, and tailor subscription tiers based on individual usage patterns. This will be a key driver for maximizing LTV in a competitive market.  
* **Expansion of Rewarded Formats:** The role of rewarded advertising (rewarded video and offerwalls) will continue to expand. These formats will be seen less as a secondary revenue stream for non-payers and more as a core component of the user engagement loop. Unity's data showing that offerwalls can increase overall LTV and even act as a gateway to IAP for non-paying users is a powerful indicator of this trend. Expect to see more sophisticated integrations of these formats even within subscription-first applications as a way to enhance value and engagement.  
* **Subscription Model Evolution:** As more apps across all categories adopt subscription models, competition for consumer wallet share will intensify. To combat "subscription fatigue," successful apps will need to evolve. This will involve incorporating hybrid elements—such as one-time purchases for premium content or consumables—to provide flexible spending options and increase total revenue per subscriber. The most successful apps will be those that can deliver continuous, demonstrable value to justify a recurring payment.

### **6.3 Actionable Recommendations for Mid-Core Game Developers**

1. **Embrace Platform-Specific Monetization from Day One:** The data is unequivocal. A one-size-fits-all monetization strategy is obsolete. Design your game's economy with bifurcated logic from the outset. For iOS, focus on a pure, uninterrupted IAP-driven experience to cater to high-spending players. For Android, build a robust hybrid system that effectively monetizes non-payers through rewarded ads to maximize your blended ROAS.  
2. **Obsess Over the First 30 Days Post-Purchase:** The window to convert a first-time buyer into a loyal, repeat spender is extremely narrow. 92% of repeat purchases happen within this first month. Your live-ops strategy must be front-loaded to recognize and nurture these new payers immediately. Implement a sequence of targeted offers, exclusive content bundles, and personalized rewards specifically for users who have just made their first purchase to build momentum and encourage further investment.  
3. **Leverage Rewarded Ads as a Strategic Tool on Android:** On Android, where hybrid monetization is key, view rewarded ads as more than just a revenue source. Use them strategically to improve core metrics. Offer rewards that help players overcome difficult levels to improve retention. Use offerwalls to allow non-paying users to "earn" premium currency, giving them a taste of the premium experience and potentially funneling them toward their first IAP, as suggested by Unity's findings.

### **6.4 Actionable Recommendations for Fitness App Publishers**

1. **Fortify the Trial Experience to Maximize Value Demonstration:** Your trial-to-paid conversion rate of 44.5% is the engine of your business. This is the single most critical phase of the user journey. Dedicate product and engineering resources to ensuring that every user experiences a clear, tangible "win" or understands the core value proposition of your app within the trial period. Whether it's completing a first workout, tracking a meal successfully, or seeing a progress chart, this initial positive feedback loop is what drives conversion.  
2. **Combat Churn and Subscription Fatigue with Gaming Mechanics:** The slight decline in annual retention to 44.1% is a warning sign that cannot be ignored. Proactively address this by integrating proven engagement strategies from the gaming world. Introduce daily check-ins, weekly fitness challenges with community leaderboards, and achievement systems with badges and rewards. These mechanics build habits, increase session frequency, and create a sense of progress and community that keeps users engaged long after their initial motivation wanes, providing a compelling reason to renew their subscription.  
3. **Experiment with a Hybrid+Subscription Model:** To increase Payer LTV and move closer to the performance of top-quartile apps, explore revenue streams beyond the core subscription. Following the broader market trend toward hybridization, experiment with offering one-time purchases for premium, high-value content. This could include personalized nutrition plans designed by experts, access to exclusive masterclass video series, or one-on-one virtual coaching sessions. These offerings provide additional value for your most engaged users and create new, high-margin revenue streams that complement your recurring subscription base.
